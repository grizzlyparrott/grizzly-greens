How Often Grass Should Be Mowed

Mowing follows growth, not the calendar

Grass should be cut based on how fast it grows.

Growth speed changes with season and weather.

The one-third rule

Removing too much leaf shocks the plant.

Frequent light cuts maintain balance.

Seasonal differences

Spring growth often requires more frequent mowing.

Summer stress slows growth and reduces frequency.

Grass type matters

Warm-season and cool-season grasses grow differently.

Each has its own active growth window.

Weather effects

Rain accelerates growth.

Drought and heat slow it down.

Mower height influence

Lower cuts require tighter mowing schedules.

Higher cuts allow longer intervals.

Signs mowing is too infrequent

Clumping and scalping increase.

Recovery time lengthens.

Consistency beats perfection

Regular adjustment matters more than exact timing.

Healthy grass tolerates minor variation.
