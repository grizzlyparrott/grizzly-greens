Why Grass Turns Yellow

Yellowing is a loss of chlorophyll

Grass appears yellow when chlorophyll production drops.

This usually happens before growth fully slows or stops.

Water stress in both directions

Underwatering limits nutrient movement and photosynthesis.

Overwatering reduces oxygen and weakens roots.

Nitrogen and nutrient imbalance

Nitrogen deficiency is a common cause of pale color.

Excess nutrients can also disrupt uptake and cause yellowing.

Soil compaction effects

Compacted soil restricts root growth.

Shallow roots struggle to support healthy leaf color.

Seasonal growth slowdowns

Grass yellows naturally when growth conditions are poor.

This often happens during heat, cold, or drought.

Mowing stress

Cutting too much leaf at once reduces energy production.

Color loss often follows heavy or frequent scalping.

Pest and disease pressure

Root damage reduces nutrient flow to blades.

Yellowing can appear before visible thinning.

What yellow color usually means

Yellow grass is stressed, not dead.

Correcting the cause usually restores color over time.
