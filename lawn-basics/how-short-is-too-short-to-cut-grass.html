How Short Is Too Short to Cut Grass

Grass grows from the base

Cutting too low removes energy-producing leaf area.

Recovery depends on remaining tissue.

Crown exposure risk

The crown sits near soil level.

Scalping exposes it to heat and drying.

Root response to low cuts

Roots shrink when leaf area is reduced.

Shallow roots increase stress sensitivity.

Heat amplification

Short grass allows soil to heat faster.

High soil temperatures slow growth.

Water loss increases

Low turf dries faster.

Irrigation demands rise.

Grass type differences

Some grasses tolerate lower cuts better.

Others decline quickly when cut short.

Repeated scalping damage

One low cut causes stress.

Repeated low cuts cause thinning.

Where the real limit is

Too short is when recovery slows.

Healthy height preserves growth and color.
