Why Grass Grows Unevenly

Soil is never uniform

Soil depth and texture vary across yards.

Roots respond directly to those differences.

Sun exposure differences

Sun warms soil unevenly.

Growth accelerates where light is strongest.

Water distribution problems

Irrigation coverage is rarely even.

Dry and wet spots grow at different speeds.

Compaction patterns

Foot traffic compresses soil in paths.

Compacted zones slow root expansion.

Root competition

Trees and shrubs steal moisture and nutrients.

Grass near roots often thins or slows.

Microclimates

Slopes, fences, and buildings change airflow.

Small climate shifts affect growth rate.

Mowing influence

Uneven cuts exaggerate growth differences.

Fast-growing areas recover first.

Why uniform lawns are rare

Perfect uniformity requires ideal conditions.

Most uneven growth reflects site reality.
