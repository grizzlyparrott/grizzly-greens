Why Grass Turns Brown

Brown means growth has stopped

Brown blades no longer photosynthesize.

The cause determines whether recovery is possible.

Drought and heat stress

Extended dryness forces grass into survival mode.

Leaf tissue dries out to protect the crown.

Natural dormancy

Some grasses turn brown to conserve energy.

Dormant grass can recover when conditions improve.

Root failure

Damaged or shallow roots cannot support growth.

This often follows overwatering or compaction.

Mowing damage

Scalping exposes crowns to heat.

Recovery slows or stops after repeated stress.

Disease and pests

Root and crown damage cut off water flow.

Brown patches often expand outward over time.

Soil and surface heat

Hardscapes raise soil temperature.

Grass near pavement browns first.

Brown does not always mean dead

Check crowns and roots before replacement.

Many lawns rebound after stress passes.
